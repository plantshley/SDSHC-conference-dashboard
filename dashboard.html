Key Insights

  • Exceptional Growth: Ticket registrations increased 56% from 251 (2022) to 391 (2025) - total 1,307+ attendees over 4 years (actual attendance likely higher with walk-ins, students, youth)
  • International Reach: Attendees from 260+ counties, 48 states & provinces, and 14 countries including Morocco, Greece, India, and Fiji
  • Consistent Excellence: 91% satisfaction rate (4-5 stars) sustained across all years despite rapid growth
  • Strong Knowledge Transfer: 85% reported high knowledge gain (3-4 out of 4), indicating effective educational content
  • Survey Response Challenge: Response rate declined from 40% (2023) to 17% (2025) as attendance grew - opportunity to improve feedback collection

Demographic Highlights

  • Diverse Stakeholder Mix: Agricultural Producers (45.5%) and Conservation Professionals (29.5%) dominate, but reaching tribal land managers, biologists, students, and industry professionals
  • 2025 Student Surge: Students & Educators jumped to 17.4% (from 4.6% in 2024), indicating growing educational pipeline and future soil health leadership

  • Conservation Professional Growth: Increased from 22.9% (2022) to 33.3% (2025), matching producers in 2025 - shows institutional adoption

Impact Analysis

  • Exceptional Implementation Rate: 97% "Likely" or "Very Likely" to implement changes (278 of 287 applicable respondents) - extraordinarily high practical impact
  • Strong Correlation: Knowledge gained, satisfaction, technician interest, and implementation likelihood are all highly correlated (p < 0.001)
  • High Satisfaction: 91% rated conference 4-5 stars, sustained across all years despite 56% attendance growth
  • Effective Knowledge Transfer: 85% reported high knowledge gain (3-4 out of 4) - suggests content is well-pitched for audience
  • Opportunity for Advanced Content: Attendees who gained more knowledge focused on tillage and cover crops - diversifying to advanced topics may benefit experienced attendees

Emerging Topics (2024-2025)

Topic Evolution Insights

  • Livestock Integration Dominates (17.2%): #1 overall topic - producers want bale grazing, rotational grazing, multi-species integration. Peaked at 25.3% (2022), stabilized at 13% (2025)
  • Cover Crops Steady (13.5%): #2 topic with consistent 11-17% interest - species selection, establishment, and grazing remain priorities
  • Soil Biology Surging (10.9%): Jumped from 4.6% (2024) to 15.9% (2025) - microbes, compost tea, biologicals gaining traction across all groups
  • Regenerative Ag Trending Up: Significant increase from 0% (2022) to 13% (2025) - statistically significant correlation with year (p=0.005)
  • Economics & Financial (8.7%): Professionals/industry prioritize economics (12-17%), while producers focus on field practices - clear audience segmentation
  • Topic Diversification: "Other Topics" grew from 12% (2022) to 39% (2024), suggesting need for varied content tracks beyond core soil health principles

Program Development Insights

  • Declining Trend (Critical): "Yes" responses dropped from 47% (2022) to 26% (2025) - needs investigation and program redesign
  • Still Substantial Interest: 35% overall (70 definite "Yes") represents solid market, but half of 2022 levels
  • Target Audience: Land managers/owners and producers most interested - professionals and students less engaged with technician program
  • Implementation Correlation: Strong positive correlation (r=0.21, p=0.002) between technician interest and likelihood to implement changes
  • Livestock Connection: Those interested in livestock integration & grazing more likely to want technician assistance (r=0.15, p=0.032)
  • Action Needed: Investigate reasons for decline - program awareness? Cost concerns? Value proposition unclear? Service delivery issues?
